# RFC-0011 — Knowledge Economics
## Prioritizing Uncertainty Reduction

Status: Draft
Depends On:
- RFC-0000 through RFC-0010

---

# Purpose

A Brain can never learn everything.

Time is limited.

Attention is limited.

Compute is limited.

Research capacity is limited.

Therefore Delphi requires a system that determines:

What should be researched next?

This RFC defines:

- Knowledge Value
- Knowledge Debt
- Research ROI
- Cost of Uncertainty
- Prioritization
- Resource Allocation

---

# Core Principle

Not all unknowns are equally important.

The purpose of Knowledge Economics is to maximize:

Knowledge Value Created

per

Unit of Effort.

---

# The Central Question

Given:

1000 unanswered questions

Which one should we investigate first?

---

# Knowledge As An Asset

Knowledge is an asset.

Knowledge can:

Increase confidence

Improve decisions

Reduce risk

Improve evaluations

Reduce future work

---

# Knowledge Value

Knowledge Value represents the benefit produced by a belief.

Examples:

Understanding gravity
→ extremely high value

Knowing tomorrow's lunch menu
→ low value

---

# Knowledge Debt

Knowledge Debt represents accumulated uncertainty.

Examples:

Missing evidence

Weak confidence

Missing evaluations

Ontology gaps

Open contradictions

Unreviewed assumptions

---

# Debt Formula

Conceptually:

Knowledge Debt

=

Importance × Uncertainty

---

# Research ROI

Research should be treated as investment.

Research ROI

=

Knowledge Value Created

÷

Research Cost

---

# Example

Question:

Can TigerBeetle survive region failure?

Potential Impact:

Very High

Research Cost:

Medium

ROI:

High

Prioritize.

---

# Cost Of Uncertainty

Every uncertainty has a cost.

Examples:

Medical Diagnosis

High Cost

Button Color Preference

Low Cost

---

# Cost Formula

Cost Of Uncertainty

≈

Probability Of Being Wrong

×

Impact Of Being Wrong

---

# Knowledge Value Dimensions

Suggested dimensions:

Decision Impact

Risk Reduction

Revenue Impact

Cost Reduction

Strategic Importance

Research Reusability

---

# Decision Impact

Question:

How many decisions depend on this belief?

More dependencies

Higher value.

---

# Dependency Economics

A belief supporting:

100 decisions

is more valuable than

a belief supporting 1 decision.

---

# Risk Reduction

Knowledge that removes risk has value.

Example:

Backup Strategy Research

may prevent disaster.

---

# Reusability

Knowledge reused many times has higher value.

Example:

Authentication Architecture

used by 50 services.

---

# Confidence Gap

Confidence Gap

=

Desired Confidence

-
Current Confidence

Large gaps increase priority.

---

# Knowledge Opportunity

Opportunity represents possible value creation.

Examples:

New Market Research

New Technology Research

Scientific Discovery

---

# Research Portfolio

Brains should maintain a portfolio.

Examples:

70%
Strategic Research

20%
Operational Research

10%
Exploratory Research

---

# Knowledge Scarcity

Knowledge resources are finite.

Examples:

Human Experts

Agent Capacity

Compute Budget

Research Time

---

# Prioritization Score

Suggested formula:

Priority

=

Impact
×
Confidence Gap
×
Dependency Count
×
Risk Reduction

÷

Estimated Cost

---

# Research Backlog

Questions should be ranked.

Examples:

Q1 Priority 95

Q2 Priority 72

Q3 Priority 31

---

# Research Queues

Suggested queues:

Critical

High

---

# Economic Signals

Brains should track:

Knowledge Debt

Confidence Growth

Research Throughput

Decision Quality

Evaluation Coverage

---

# Research Waste

Waste occurs when:

Question already answered

Research duplicated

Low-value uncertainty explored

Evidence ignored

---

# Knowledge Compounding

Knowledge compounds.

Example:

Understanding databases

helps:

Storage

Search

Caching

Analytics

Architecture

Knowledge with compounding effects should be prioritized.

---

# Knowledge Monopoly

Some knowledge is difficult to acquire.

Examples:

Rare expertise

Proprietary research

Specialized domains

Such knowledge has higher value.

---

# Knowledge Markets

Future Vision:

Brains may exchange:

Research

Evidence

Capabilities

Evaluations

Knowledge Value becomes measurable.

---

# Agent Prioritization

Agents should not choose research randomly.

Agents should maximize:

Expected Knowledge Value.

---

# Success Criteria

A Delphi system successfully implements this RFC when:

1. Knowledge debt is measurable.
2. Research is prioritized.
3. Uncertainty has cost.
4. Research ROI is estimated.
5. Dependencies influence priority.
6. Confidence gaps are visible.
7. Research waste is reduced.
8. Knowledge value is measurable.
9. Agents prioritize intelligently.
10. The brain learns the most valuable things first.
